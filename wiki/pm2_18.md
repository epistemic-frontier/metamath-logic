# pm2.18 — Clavius Law (consequentia mirabilis)

**Statement:** `(¬φ → φ) → φ`

If a formula is implied by its negation, then it is true.

## Historical Notes

Known as "Clavius law" or "consequentia mirabilis" ("admirable consequence").
First noted by medieval logicians and later formalized by Christoph Clavius (1538–1612).

## Proof Sketch

The proof uses modus ponens and contraposition to derive φ from ¬φ → φ.
